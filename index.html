Full Production Sequence

Every stage executed.
Every parameter justified.

Stage 01
Material Preparation
Al7075 + SiC reinforcement
Selected base alloy and reinforcement composition. Prepared material batches at varying reinforcement weight percentages to establish the experimental matrix. Composition ratios justified against target mechanical properties.
Parameter selection
Stage 02
Stir Casting
6kW furnace · stirring speed controlled
Cast specimens in a 6kW resistance furnace. Controlled and recorded furnace temperature, stirring speed, stirring duration, and mold setup for each specimen. Stir casting selected for its ability to produce uniform reinforcement distribution at this scale.
Furnace operation · real
Stage 03
Heat Treatment
Duration and temp. selected per specimen
Applied T6-style heat treatment sequence. Selected and justified heat treatment temperature and duration for each specimen based on alloy composition. Parameters recorded for ANOVA inclusion. Same judgment applied by a process engineer qualifying a new component.
Process parameter judgment
Stage 04
Machining to Dimensions
Test-ready surface finish
Machined all 12 specimens to standard test dimensions for wear and hardness characterization. Surface preparation controlled to eliminate machining-induced variability from the wear test results.
Dimensional control
Stage 05
Wear & Hardness Testing
Pin-on-disc · Rockwell hardness
Characterized all specimens for wear rate and hardness. Wear testing conducted under controlled load, speed, and duration conditions. Hardness measured at multiple points per specimen. Results tabulated for statistical analysis.
Experimental data
Stage 06
ANOVA + Regression
R² = 0.96 · optimal composition identified
Applied ANOVA to identify significant process parameters and interaction effects. Regression model (R² = 0.96) quantified the relationship between composition variables and wear performance. Optimal composition identified with statistical confidence.
R² = 0.96 model fit
Every parameter was chosen.
None were assumed.
Each stage of this project required selecting process parameters before running the next stage — furnace temperature, stirring speed, mold configuration, heat treatment duration. This is the same engineering judgment a manufacturing or process engineer applies when developing or modifying a production process for a new component. The difference is that here it was done on physical specimens in a real fabrication environment, with results verified statistically through ANOVA. R² = 0.96 means the model explains 96% of the variance in wear performance — a strong fit for physical experimentation.

Why This Appears in an Analytics Portfolio
This project demonstrates that the analytical tools used in the graduate projects — regression, ANOVA, model validation — were learned in a context where the data was physically produced by hand, not downloaded. The R² = 0.96 result came from 12 specimens fabricated in a 6kW furnace, not from a clean CSV file.

For roles in manufacturing operations, materials, process engineering, or any environment where analytical work touches physical production — this project establishes that the methodology is understood at its root, not just applied to synthetic data.
